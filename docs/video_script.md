# Y - 3-minute hackathon video

A scene-by-scene shooting script for the YouTube submission. Keep total
duration under 3:00. Every "(beat)" is a paragraph break in narration.

---

## Cold open · 0:00 - 0:12 (12 s)

**Visual.** Phone-camera shot of a child at a desk with their textbook,
working through a homework problem alone. Audio: just pencil scratches,
maybe the kid muttering "I don't get this." 4 seconds.

Cut to **black slate**: white text on black,

> Two billion children. One textbook each.
> Most have no one to ask.

3 seconds, hold.

**Narrator (VO, calm).**
> AI is rewriting how we work. It still hasn't reached how we *learn*.
> Most AI tutors are chat boxes. Most kids don't learn from chat boxes.
> They learn at a whiteboard, with someone who can draw.

(beat)

> So we built that someone.

---

## Title card · 0:12 - 0:18 (6 s)

Show the **logo** + tagline:

> **Y** - the AI that writes on your whiteboard.
>
> Powered by Gemma 4. Ollama. Unsloth.

(Use the demo color palette: emerald accent on white. Tagline fades up
with light rough-sketch animation under the **Y**, matching the
hand-drawn aesthetic of the canvas.)

---

## Demo 1 · Physics · 0:18 - 0:55 (37 s)

**Visual.** Screen recording. Open <http://localhost:3000>, blank
Excalidraw canvas. Cursor moves to the **Physics** sample button, click.
A hand-drawn incline, block, and "a = ?" appear at top-left of the
canvas.

**Narrator.**
> A 9th-grader writes a free-body problem on the whiteboard.

Cursor hovers the **Solve** button. Click.

(beat - 2 s of silence so the user hears the model wake.)

**Narrator.**
> Y reads the canvas. The student marked the unknown with a question
> mark - that's how Y knows what to teach.

The right-hand side of the canvas starts filling in:

1. Title `"Frictionless incline · find a"` writes itself letter-by-letter,
   synced to TTS.
2. Equation `g · sin θ` renders via KaTeX.
3. A **part-structured SVG** of the block + incline strokes-on, segment
   by segment - "Drawing the incline." then "Drawing the block.", with
   each path animating in.
4. Final caption: `a = 9.8 · sin 30° = 4.9 m/s²`.

**Narrator (over the strokes).**
> Notice it's not pasting an image. Every stroke is generated by the
> model, validated, and drawn as you watch. That's the SVG-native
> primitive we added on top of Gemma 4.

End the demo on the green checkmark next to the answer.

## Demo 2 · Chemistry · 0:55 - 1:25 (30 s)

**Visual.** Click **Chem** sample. Canvas shows "Draw the structure of
benzene? C6H6 · structure = ?".

**Narrator.**
> Same canvas. Different subject.

Click **Solve**. The tutor draws the **benzene hexagon** ring, places
the alternating double bonds, labels each carbon and hydrogen, all in
sequence narrated as "Drawing the ring", "Adding the double bonds",
"Labeling the atoms."

**Narrator.**
> Six carbons, six hydrogens, three double bonds, alternating. The
> drawing primitive is constrained by a schema, sanitized for unsafe
> SVG, and salvaged when paths fail. Most edge inference is fragile.
> Ours degrades gracefully.

(End on a clean benzene + the equation `C₆H₆`.)

## Demo 3 · Learner profile · 1:25 - 2:00 (35 s)

**Visual.** Bottom-left LearnerPanel comes into focus. Pan / zoom in.

**Narrator.**
> Every lesson, Y extracts the concepts the student just touched -
> *Pythagorean theorem*, *vector resolution*, *aromaticity* - embeds
> them with a local nomic model, and saves them on disk.

UMAP scatter starts spinning - 3D point cloud, sessions colored
chronologically.

**Narrator.**
> This is the student's knowledge map. After three sessions, Y already
> knows what they've mastered, what they're struggling with, and what
> to teach next. The mastery summary becomes the prefix of the next
> system prompt - so the lesson actually adapts.

Hover one point: tooltip pops up with topic + concepts.

**Narrator.**
> No accounts. No cloud. The profile is JSON on the device. The student
> *owns* the model of themselves.

**Narrator.**
> Y runs three Gemma 4 models behind one whiteboard. The big cloud one

> way humans do, not the way fine-tuners do.

Cut briefly to the toolbar dropdown showing the three options.

## Recording checklist

- [ ] Shoot the cold-open + closer with a real child or stand-in.
- [ ] Laptop microphone is bad; record narration separately on phone
      voice memo + level it in Audacity (`amplify` then `compressor`).
- [ ] All three demos in sequence with `npm run dev` + uvicorn already
      warm. Use `--all-models` smoke ahead of the recording session so
      Ollama has cached weights.
- [ ] Use the **physics** sample first (it's the most visually striking
      with the inclined plane).
- [ ] Cap the canvas zoom at 100% so text doesn't blur in compression.
- [ ] Export at 1080p, 30 fps, h264. YouTube re-encodes anyway.

## Pacing notes

- Aim for ≤ 3:00 total. Hard cut anything that pushes past.
- The narration must end *before* the visuals do; never cut narration
  mid-word.
- Two beats of silence between scenes so the viewer feels the model
  "thinking" instead of being talked over the whole time.
